SHOW RESULTS - 12/8 WWE in Spokane, Wash.: Cena vs. Del Rio caps tour, Kofi-Miz, U.S. Title & Tag Title matches

Dec 9, 2013 - 12:41:01 AM

WWE house show results; December 8, 2013; Spokane, Wash.; Report by Alexander Bell, PWTorch reader

I took my son and daughter to the show, and it was my 6-year-old son’s first show. He was stoked. We got there and they upgraded our seats. Only about 2,500 people were there, maybe. Bad storylines are hurting WWE, in my opinion.

(8) WWE World Hvt. champion John Cena beat Alberto Del Rio to retain World Title. Cena hasn’t been to Spokane in a few years, so he got a massive pop. Typical Cena match. It was entertaining, but pretty much his same few moves. I like Cena and would love to see the version of him from Summerslam much more.

Overall, it was a decent show. It was awesome for me because my kids had a blast. A Cena show brings out the kids and there were a lot there. I hope they don’t wait a year to come back again… or Ring of Honor starts coming west. Now that would be awesome.

WWE Live Event Results From Spokane (12/8): Cena Vs, Del Rio, Ambrose Defends In Triple Threat Match

By Raj Giri | December 09, 2013

Thanks to WrestlingINC.com readers Rachel Miller and Alex for sending in these results from last night’s WWE live event in Spokane, WA:

There were about 5000 people in attendance. Before the show starts, fans had a chance to vote for a divas dance off or a divas tag team match. Justin Roberts comes out a gives a warm welcome.

John Cena defeated Alberto Del Rio to retain the World Heavyweight Championship. The highlight was when Del Rio took a “Cena Sucks” sign from a fan. Cena was very over. At one point in the match, the referee got knocked to the floor as Cena made Del Rio tap. Del Rio then used the belt to hit Cena and then he dragged the ref into the ring, but only to get a slow 2 1/2 count. Cena then nailed Del Rio with the AA to win the match.

No return date has been announced.

Biggest pops: 1. John Cena 2. Rhodes & Goldust 3. Kofi Kingston

Most heat: 1. Alberto Del Rio 2. Damien Sandow 3. Ryback
